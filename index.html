Types of cyber attacks





Malware (short for “malicious software”) is a file or code,
typically delivered over a network, that
infects, explores, steals or conducts virtually any behavior an attacker wants.






Phishing is a type of social engineering attack often used to steal user data, including login credentials and credit card numbers. It occurs when an attacker, masquerading as a trusted entity, dupes a victim into opening an email, instant message, or text message.






Identity theft, is a crime in which an imposter obtains key pieces of personally identifiable information (PII) to impersonate someone else.






Unethical hacking refers to the use of advanced technology for exploiting weaknesses and breaching defences in a computer system.






Cyber bullying is the use of electronic communication to bully a person, typically by sending messages of an intimidating or threatening nature.






Online piracy is the practice of downloading and distributing copyrighted content digitally without permission, such as music or software.

HACKING

Hacking is not always a malicious act, but it is most commonly associated with illegal activity and data theft by cyber criminals.

There are 3 types of hackers -
Ethical or White Hat Hackers (legal) who are helpful to companies/government to secure their digital domain.
Grey Hat hackers (legal/illegal) who may/may not help for the right cause
Black hat or Unethical hackers (illegal) who misuse the internet and steal information
